What are Semlor?

Semlor are traditional Swedish buns filled with almond paste and whipped cream. They are typically eaten in Sweden during Lent and on Shrove Tuesday.

History

The origins of semlor can be traced back to medieval times, when they were a simple bun made of flour and water. Over time, they became a symbol of wealth and luxury, as richer ingredients such as milk and butter were added. Today, they are a beloved Swedish tradition.

Recipe

Here is a simple recipe for semlor:

  1. Mix together flour, sugar, salt, and yeast in a large bowl.
  2. Add warm milk and melted butter to the bowl, and stir until the dough comes together.
  3. Knead the dough on a floured surface for 10-15 minutes, until it is smooth and elastic.
  4. Cover the dough and let it rise in a warm place for about an hour, until it has doubled in size.
  5. Punch down the dough and divide it into 12 equal pieces. Shape each piece into a ball and place them on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  6. Cover the dough balls and let them rise for another 30-45 minutes.
  7. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  8. Brush the tops of the dough balls with beaten egg, and bake them in the oven for 12-15 minutes, until they are golden brown.
  9. Let the buns cool completely before slicing off the tops and scooping out some of the inside to make room for the almond paste and whipped cream filling.
  10. Mix together almond paste, sugar, and milk to make the filling, and spoon it into the buns. Top with whipped cream and replace the tops of the buns.

Where to Buy

Semlor can be found at most bakeries and cafes in Sweden during the Lenten season. If you can't make it to Sweden, you can try making your own semlor at home with the recipe above!
